About

Hi there! My name is John Reniel D. Balsacao, and I’m a 19-year-old BSIT student at Carlos Hilado Memorial State University, currently based in Bacolod City. I was born on November 2, 2005, in Sagay City, and I now live in a boarding house in Brgy. Alijis while studying at my university.

Although my academic focus is on Information Technology, where I am learning a variety of programming languages such as Java, JavaScript, C, and more, my passions extend far beyond the world of code. I’ve always had a strong love for the arts, and it has been a part of me for as long as I can remember. Whether it’s drawing, painting, or other forms of creative expression, I find peace and inspiration through my artistic side.

Alongside my academic and artistic pursuits, I have a deep love for music, especially playing the guitar. Music has been a huge part of my life, offering a creative outlet and a way to express my thoughts and emotions. I enjoy experimenting with different melodies and chords during my free time, and it often serves as a way to unwind after a busy day of coding and studying.

As I navigate my way through the path of becoming an IT professional, I’m constantly exploring new technologies, tools, and frameworks that allow me to expand my skills. I find it fascinating how technology can shape our world, and I’m eager to be a part of that evolution. But what truly excites me is the potential to combine my love for art and technology. Whether it’s through web development, game design, or creative coding, I aim to use my skills to create digital experiences that are both functional and visually engaging.

When I’m not studying, coding, or playing music, you can find me spending time with friends, exploring new hobbies, or simply reflecting on ways to grow both personally and professionally. The journey ahead is exciting, and I can’t wait to see where my passion for IT and art will take me.

My aim is to become an expert in blending technology with artistry to produce digital experiences that leave a lasting impact.

Ultimately, my vision is to use my skills to create a positive impact in the world.
